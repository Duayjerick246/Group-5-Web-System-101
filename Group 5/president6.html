Corazon Aquino

Background: Born on January 25, 1933, in Manila, Corazon Aquino was a prominent political figure and the first female president of the Philippines.

Biography: Aquino became president in 1986 after the People Power Revolution, which ousted Ferdinand Marcos. Her presidency was marked by efforts to restore democracy and rebuild a nation recovering from dictatorship.

Contributions:

  • Democratic Restoration: Worked to reinstate democratic processes and institutions.
  • Constitutional Reform: Oversaw the drafting of the 1987 Constitution, establishing a new democratic framework.
  • Economic Policies: Promoted agrarian reform and initiatives to boost local industries.

Highlights: First Female President, Restorer of Democracy, Advocate for Social Justice

Fidel V. Ramos

Background: Born on March 18, 1928, in Lingayen, Pangasinan, Ramos served as a general in the Philippine Army before entering politics.

Biography: Serving from 1992 to 1998, Ramos was known for his efforts to stabilize the economy and modernize the military. His administration focused on promoting infrastructure and good governance.

Contributions:

  • Economic Growth: Implemented policies that led to significant economic growth during his term.
  • Infrastructure Development: Launched major projects to improve transportation and public services.
  • Peace Initiatives: Worked towards peace agreements with various rebel groups, contributing to national stability.

Highlights: Economic Modernization, Infrastructure Development, Peace Advocate
