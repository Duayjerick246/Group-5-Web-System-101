Manuel Roxas

Background: Manuel Roxas was born on January 1, 1892, in Capiz. He served as a politician and was instrumental in the transition to independence after World War II.

Biography: Roxas became the first President of the independent Third Republic of the Philippines, serving from 1946 until his death in 1948. He focused on post-war recovery and the establishment of a stable government.

Contributions:

  • Post-War Reconstruction: Worked on rebuilding the country after the devastation of World War II.
  • Economic Development: Initiated policies for economic recovery and development.
  • Strengthening US Relations: Fostered close ties with the United States for support in rebuilding efforts.

Highlights: First President of the Third Republic, Advocate for Post-War Recovery

Elpidio Quirino

Background: Elpidio Quirino was born on November 16, 1890, in Vigan, Ilocos Sur. He was a lawyer and a politician, serving in various government positions before becoming president.

Biography: Quirino served as the second President of the independent Third Republic from 1948 to 1953. His leadership was marked by efforts to promote economic recovery and improve infrastructure.

Contributions:

  • Economic Recovery: Implemented policies to boost the economy and recover from the war.
  • Infrastructure Development: Focused on building roads, schools, and hospitals.
  • Social Welfare Programs: Established programs to assist the poor and displaced citizens.

Highlights: Advocate for Economic Recovery, Focused on Infrastructure Development
